# The Billion Dollar Secret

Every year, millions of passengers are delayed, stranded, or bumped from flights. And every year, **billions of Euros** in legally mandated compensation go unclaimed. Why? Because the airlines rely on two powerful weapons: **Ignorance** and **Exhaustion**.

They bank on you not knowing your rights under **Regulation (EC) No 261/2004**. And if you do know them, they bank on you giving up after the third ignored email or the fourth hour on hold.

This manifesto is your weapon. It is the definitive guide to turning a travel disaster into a profitable venture.

## The Legal Framework: EC 261/2004

In 2004, the European Parliament passed a piece of legislation that changed aviation forever. It established that passengers are not just cargo, but humans with rights to punctuality and care.

### Jurisdiction: Does This Apply to Me?

The regulation applies to a surprisingly broad range of flights. It covers:
1.  **Any flight** departing from an EU/EEA airport (regardless of the airline).
2.  **Any flight** arriving at an EU/EEA airport, IF operated by an EU/EEA carrier.

**Scenario A:** You fly United Airlines from New York (JFK) to London (LHR).
*   *Verdict:* **NOT Covered** (Non-EU carrier, departing non-EU).
*   *Correction:* If you flew British Airways on the same route, you **WOULD** be covered.

**Scenario B:** You fly United Airlines from London (LHR) to New York (JFK).
*   *Verdict:* **Covered** (Departing EU airport).

### The Three Pillars of Compensation

The regulation covers three main disruptions:
1.  **Delay:** Arriving at your final destination more than 3 hours late.
2.  **Cancellation:** Flight cancelled less than 14 days before departure.
3.  **Denied Boarding:** Being bumped due to overbooking (involuntary).

## The Payout Structure

The compensation is fixed. It is not based on your ticket price. You could buy a €20 Ryanair ticket and receive €250 in compensation.

| Distance | Compensation | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Short Haul** (< 1,500 km) | **€250** | London - Paris, Berlin - Milan |
| **Medium Haul** (1,500 - 3,500 km) | **€400** | London - Athens, Madrid - Moscow |
| **Long Haul** (> 3,500 km) | **€600** | London - New York, Paris - Tokyo |

## The Enemy's Tactics: "Extraordinary Circumstances"

Article 5(3) of the regulation states that an airline is exempt from paying if the disruption was caused by *extraordinary circumstances which could not have been avoided even if all reasonable measures had been taken.*

This is the loophole airlines abuse. They will call **everything** extraordinary.

### The Lie Detector

**LIE:** "Technical Fault."
**TRUTH:** The European Court of Justice (ECJ) has ruled repeatedly that technical maintenance issues are inherent to running an airline. They are **NOT** extraordinary. They are operational failures.

**LIE:** "Crew Sickness."
**TRUTH:** Airlines are required to have reserve crews. Staff sickness is an operational issue, not a force majeure event.

**LIE:** "Knock-on Effects."
**TRUTH:** "The plane was late arriving from the previous city because of weather." If your flight is delayed because of weather *somewhere else* hours ago, that is often considered an operational planning failure, though this is a grey area where legal teams fight.

### Valid Excuses (When You Don't Get Paid)

*   **Actual Weather:** Thunderstorms closing the airport, heavy snow, low visibility.
*   **ATC Strikes:** Air Traffic Control strikes are outside airline control.
*   **Security Threats:** Bomb scares, runway incursions, political unrest.

## The Right to Care (Article 9)

Even if the delay IS extraordinary (e.g., a blizzard), the airline strictly owes you a **Right to Care**. They cannot leave you sleeping on a floor.

If the delay is significant, they MUST provide:
1.  **Meals and Refreshments:** Vouchers for food.
2.  **Communication:** Access to phone/email.
3.  **Accommodation:** A hotel room if an overnight stay is required.
4.  **Transport:** Taxi/bus to the hotel and back.

**The Golden Rule:** If they refuse to help (which they often do when overwhelmed), **pay for it yourself and keep every single receipt**. They are legally required to reimburse reasonable expenses. Do not buy champagne and caviar, but a decent meal and a 3-star hotel are fully claimable.

## The Protocol: How to Execute a Claim

### Phase 1: At the Airport
1.  **Document Everything:** Take photos of the departure board showing the delay. Screenshot the airline app.
2.  **Demand Reasons:** Ask the gate agent *why* the flight is delayed. If possible, record audio or get it in writing.
3.  **Boarding Passes:** Never throw them away. Digital versions are fine, but save the PDF or screenshot.

### Phase 2: The Approach
You have two options.

**Option A: The Lone Wolf (Manual)**
You can write to the airline yourself.
*   *Pros:* You keep 100% of the money.
*   *Cons:* It is painful. They will ignore you. Then they will lie to you ("extraordinary circumstances"). Then they will offer you a voucher worth 50% of the value. You may need to take them to small claims court.

**Option B: The Mercenary (Automated)**
Use a service like AirHelp or FlightRight.
*   *Pros:* Zero stress. They verify the weather data and flight logs. They have lawyers who sue airlines in bulk.
*   *Cons:* They take a cut (usually 30-35%).
*   *Verdict:* If you value your time, use a service. 65% of €600 is better than 100% of €0.

How to Claim EU261 Compensation Like a Pro

AuthorTravelKing Intelligence
Authored DateFebruary 08, 2026

Don't Let Them Keep Your Money

If you've flown from an EU/UK airport, or on an EU/UK airline to one, you are protected by EC 261/2004 or UK 261. These regulations are the gold standard of passenger rights.

When Are You Eligible?

  • Delay: Arrival at final destination is delayed by 3+ hours.
  • Cancellation: Flight cancelled less than 14 days before departure.
  • Denied Boarding: Overbooking.

How Much Can You Get?

The compensation is fixed based on flight distance:

DistanceCompensation
< 1,500 km€250
1,500 - 3,500 km€400
> 3,500 km€600

The "Extraordinary Circumstances" Loophole

Airlines will almost always claim "extraordinary circumstances" (weather, ATC strikes) to avoid paying.

Tactical Advice:

  • Check the weather reports yourself.
  • If other flights are landing, it's not the weather.
  • "Technical issues" are NOT extraordinary circumstances. They are the airline's responsibility.

Automated Recovery

Don't waste time fighting legal teams yourself. Use automated services like AirHelp to handle the bureaucracy. They take a cut, but they win cases you would lose.

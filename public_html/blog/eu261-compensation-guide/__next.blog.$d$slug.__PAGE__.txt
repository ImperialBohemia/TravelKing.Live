<p>If you've flown from an EU/UK airport, or on an EU/UK airline to one, you are protected by EC 261/2004 or UK 261. These regulations are the gold standard of passenger rights.</p>
<h2>When Are You Eligible?</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Delay:</strong> Arrival at final destination is delayed by 3+ hours.</li>
<li><strong>Cancellation:</strong> Flight cancelled less than 14 days before departure.</li>
<li><strong>Denied Boarding:</strong> Overbooking.</li>
</ul>

<h2>The "Extraordinary Circumstances" Loophole</h2>
<p>Airlines will almost <em>always</em> claim "extraordinary circumstances" (weather, ATC strikes) to avoid paying.</p>
<p><strong>Tactical Advice:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Check the weather reports yourself.</li>
<li>If other flights are landing, it's not the weather.</li>
<li>"Technical issues" are <strong>NOT</strong> extraordinary circumstances. They are the airline's responsibility.</li>
</ul>
